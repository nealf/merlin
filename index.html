What is National Day of Civic Hacking?

On June 6, 2015, thousands of people from across the United States will come together for National Day of Civic Hacking. The event will bring together urbanists, civic hackers, government staff, developers, designers, community organizers and anyone with the passion to make their city better. They will collaboratively build new solutions using publicly-released data, technology, and design processes to improve our communities and the governments that serve them. Anyone can participate; you don't have to be an expert in technology, you just have to care about your neighborhood and community.

Join Code for NRV as we hold our own event with projects and solutions relevant to our region!

About Us

We are Code for New River Valley, an official Brigade Chapter of Code for America. We started in December 2014 with a mission statement to "promote open data, open access, and civic engagement in the New River Valley by collaborating with local governments, community organizations, and citizens to develop applications, create tools, and find technology solutions to challenges of all sizes."

We welcome people of all types, and contrary to our name, you don't have to be a programmer to participate! We're just looking for people who are inspired and motivated to help their local communities. We host weekly "hacknights" where people get together to code, talk about ideas, and find technology related solutions to challenges in our area and region. Come to one of our other events to get involved even more!

This event, along with all of our events, follows our Code of Conduct to create a welcoming and safe environment. If you ever have any questions or problems, please don't hesitate to contact us!
